Unit 1 - At the Zoo



Overview
In this unit, students will delve into the fascinating world of zoo animals while reinforcing their understanding of the present simple tense. They will learn to describe animals, their habitats, behaviors, and characteristics using appropriate vocabulary and grammar structures.

Objectives
To learn and use vocabulary related to zoo animals.
To practice and apply the present simple tense.
To describe animals using detailed sentences.
To enhance listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills through interactive activities.
Topics Covered
Vocabulary: Zoo Animals

Introduction to various zoo animals (e.g., lion, elephant, giraffe, zebra, penguin).
Learning animal names, characteristics, habitats, and behaviors.
Grammar: Present Simple

Forming present simple sentences.
Affirmative, negative, and interrogative forms.
Usage in habitual actions and general facts.
Describing Animals

Using adjectives to describe animals (e.g., big, small, fast, slow, furry).
Forming sentences to describe animals' appearances, habitats, and behaviors.
Comparative and superlative forms (e.g., bigger, fastest).
